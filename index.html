The Trip

A weekend that's worth the drive.

This isn't a brand-new idea. We've done this trip before. The format works.

Lake Morey is a third-generation family-owned resort in Vermont's Connecticut River Upper Valley — easy I-91 access, a championship 18-hole course with a flat front nine and a mountainous back, lake views, and the kind of fall foliage you write home about.

Our group is mostly retired firefighters, with police, EMS, and veterans rounding it out — most of us in our 50s and 60s, all of us still chasing a little white ball around. We come up Friday afternoon, play a quick nine, sleep, play 36 on Saturday, sleep, play 18 Sunday, then drive home tired and happy.

The window we're targeting is mid-September to the second week of October. We're focused on Friday – Sunday but open to other days if it helps with pricing or availability.

Climate norms, not a forecast.

For your gut-check on Oct 16–18. These are typical-year weather and foliage references for Fairlee / Upper Connecticut River Valley — not a forecast (way too early). Use to set expectations and pack lists, not to make weather promises.

Typical mid-October weather Climate normals

  • Average high~54°F (12°C)
  • Average low~40°F (4°C)
  • Overnight frost riskReal — Sunday 7 AM exposure
  • October monthly rainfall~3.1" (wettest month of year)
  • Days w/ measurable precip~14 of 31 in October
Translation: bring a layer and a rain shell. Cool and crisp days, cold mornings. Real frost-delay potential on the Sunday early window — worth asking Shanna about their frost-delay protocol before locking it.

Typical mid-October foliage CT River Valley

  • Vermont overallAt peak or just past
  • Upper CT River Valley (Fairlee)Sugar maples turning fast; some leaf drop
  • Lower CT River ValleyBright reds — red maple holding
  • Best color in this region~Oct 5 – 15 typical
  • Oct 16 – 18 statusLate peak / early drop
Translation: we're catching the back end of peak in our specific spot. Less postcard than the first weekend of October, but still solidly colorful — bright maples mixed with some bare branches. Year-to-year variation is real.
